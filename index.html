What is AI ?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) works by simulating human intelligence through the use of algorithms, data, and computational power. The goal is to enable machines or software to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and language understanding. AI is a machine's ability to perform the cognitive functions we associate with human minds, such as perceiving, reasoning, learning, interacting with the environment, problem-solving, and even exercising creativity.AI is free, as open source. The thing is that you don't have the sufficient hardware to run them on your local systems.Artificial intelligence is the simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems. Examples of AI applications include expert systems, natural language processing (NLP), speech recognition and machine vision.

How dose it work?

In general, AI systems work by ingesting large amounts of labeled training data, analyzing that data for correlations and patterns, and using these patterns to make predictions about future states.
1. Collect data AI systems collect large amounts of labeled training data.
2. Apply data to algorithms The data is applied to mathematical models, or algorithms, which use the information to recognize patterns.
3. Train the algorithms The algorithms learn from the patterns and features in the data. This process can involve progressive learning algorithms that let the data do the programming. For example, an algorithm can teach itself to play chess or recommend products online.
4. Deploy the algorithms Once trained, the algorithms are used in various applications, where they can continuously learn from and adapt to new data. AI systems can simulate how the brain works and use different subfields, such as machine learning. Machine learning automates analytical model building to find hidden insights in data without being programmed to look for something specific. AI systems can be used for many purposes, including: Fraud detection, Robots for dangerous jobs, Healthcare research, and Climate initiatives.
